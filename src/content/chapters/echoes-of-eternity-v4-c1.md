# Chapter 1: Smoke and Thunder

The battlefield was chaos. Musket fire cracked like thunder, cannons roared, and men died by the hundreds. Captain Kael Thornton led his company through the smoke, his sword—an anachronism in this age of gunpowder—flashing in the dim light.

He fought like a man possessed, like a man who had fought a thousand battles before.

Because he had.
